Welcome to Company Speak Up

Company Speak Up is an independent whistleblowing service managed by Service Provider Singapore, giving you the opportunity to 'Speak Up' on impropriety or misdeeds. We want to ensure Company continues to be a safe, ethical and honest workplace.

Fraud and misconduct has a negative impact on Company’ workplace and reputation. By reporting such activity you can help ensure Company continues to be a safe, ethical and honest place to work.

Most reported cases are uncovered through tips from honest people who may not be sure of all the facts - we do not expect you to know every detail. Reporting what you do know is a sufficient enough first step. However, please note that all reporting should be made in good faith and without malicious intent or for personal gains.

Company Speak up provides two channels to make a report, by website or call.

Your identity is protected and will be kept confidential by Service Provider, to the extent feasible and permissible under the law or unless you choose to reveal it. Company Speak Up provides an option for you to remain anonymous or disclose your identity.

Once you have provided your information to Company Speak Up, a report will be made and forwarded to the representative(s) in Company who is responsible for addressing fraud and misconduct, for their onward investigation.

Company Speak Up may contact you for more information, if required (if you have provided your contact details). Also, if you have any new or additional information, you can make a follow up report, uoting the reference number that was initially provided to you.

Don't turn a ‘blind eye’ - Speak Up and help continue to make Company a safe, ethical and honest workplace.

What to Report

If you see any suspicious activity, illegal or unethical behaviour in Company, ‘Speak Up’ and report it. Such activity or behaviour could include:

  1. Breaches of Professional Integrity / Unethical Behaviour
  2. Breach of Banking Secrecy
  3. Conflict of Interest
  4. Data Leakage / Information Theft
  5. Unauthorised System Access
  6. Criminal Offences where A Staff is Involved
  7. Regulatory Breach

Examples of suspicious activity:

1. Breaches of Professional Integrity / Unethical Behaviour

  • Where a Company employee does not act in good faith for Company or does not behave in a manner that promotes trust.
  • Where a Company employee abuses his/her position or authority for an unfair advantage or benefit, or does so to the detriment of interests to other parties.

2. Breach of Banking Secrecy

  • Where a Company employee does not maintain the confidentiality of customer's information by disclosing or publishing information relating to customer accounts' affairs and conduct to another party.

3. Conflict of Interest

  • Where a Company employee holds any undeclared position in another company having dealings with Company.
  • Where a Company employee has family members or friends in business relationships with Company and does not disclose it.
  • Where a Company employee receives any financial or non-financial gifts or entertainment from a vendor/supplier and recommends to award the contract to the vendor/supplier without performing a proper quotation analysis against other vendors/suppliers.

4. Data Leakage / Information Theft

  • Where a Company employee involves in transmitting data, electronically or physically from within the organisation to external recipient without authorisation or consent.
  • Where a Company employee leaks any information about Company such as budgets, vendor quotation, and confidential information to other unauthorised people.
  • Where a Company employee transmits company data for personal purposes and/or benefits.

5. Unauthorised System Access

  • Where a Company employee accesses into a Company computer terminal or data system for non-official purposes and/or without authorisation.

6. Staff Involvement in Criminal activities

  • Where a Company employee violates the law or involves in the activities that are punishable by law such as Bribery & Corruption, Financial Statement Fraud, Forgery, Cheating, Insider Trading, Theft of Assets, unlawful gambling etc.

7. Regulatory Breach

  • Where a Company employee's actions violates Company's operating and licensing obligations to any regulatory authority.

8. Other Breaches

The following are breaches which if reported, may be investigated by other departments and organizations:

  • Human resources-related cases - Workplace concerns, Employee grievances, harassment cases, etc.
  • External Fraud - wrongdoing or fraud committed by non-employees of Company (e.g. Company' customers, 3rd party fraudsters, etc).

Frequently Asked Questions

FAQ 1: Why should I Speak Up on fraud and misconduct?

To help continue to make Company a safe and honest place to work. Fraud and misconduct does have a negative effect on our workplace and reputation. By reporting fraud and misconduct you can help to provide a safe and honest environment and enhance the effectiveness of Company.

FAQ 2: Can I remain anonymous?

It's all up to you whether you choose to remain anonymous or not. All whistleblowing reports will be investigated regardless of whether they are anonymous, provided that there is sufficient information. However, by fully disclosing your identity, Company will be able to contact you to clarify relevant matters or obtain further information that may aid in the investigation.

At times, you may not be able to remain anonymous (even if you choose to do so) as your identity may be apparent from the information given. Regardless of whether you choose to reveal your identity or remain anonymous, we will take all reasonable steps to maintain confidentiality and protect your identity. Every effort will be made not to reveal your identity to the extent feasible and permissible under the law.

FAQ 3: What happens when I make a call?

You will not be asked by Company Speak Up to provide your name unless you wish to identify yourself. Your report will be known only by a unique reference number, unless you choose to tell Company Speak Up your name.

You will be asked to provide your detailed report to an experienced and independent forensic investigator, who may ask you some additional clarification questions, while taking the details of your report.

FAQ 4: What do I need to tell Company SpeakUp?

Tell Company Speak Up as much information as you can when you Speak Up. For example:

  • Names of people involved;
  • Names of any witnesses (if any);
  • Your name and email address;
  • Details of evidence you know of;
  • The value of any money or asset involved; and
  • How often this incident occurred.

FAQ 5: What if i suspect something is wrong but I am not sure?

Most cases reported are uncovered through tips from honest people who may not be sure of all the facts. This is perfectly all right. We do not expect you will to know every last detail. Reporting what you do know is enough.
